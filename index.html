INNOVATIVE WATER SAVING TECHNIQUES

Rainwater harvesting includes several effective techniques to manage and conserve water resources.

Rooftop harvesting:

Rooftop Harvesting collects rainwater from building roofs and stores it for household or commercial use. This involves the collection of the rainwater that falls on the roof area of the buildings, which is then diverted to a rainwater filter through a downspout pipe.

Recharge pits:

Recharge pits are shallow, dug-out structures that allow rainwater to slowly seep into the ground, replenishing groundwater levels. They help prevent waterlogging, reduce surface runoff, and support sustainable water management in homes, farms, and urban areas.

Percolation tanks:

Percolation tanks are small to medium-sized reservoirs that store rainwater and allow it to slowly infiltrate into the soil. They help recharge groundwater, control floods, and ensure a steady water supply for agriculture and communities during dry periods.

Surface runoff harvesting:

Surface runoff harvesting captures excess rainwater flowing over land and paved areas. This water can be directed into storage tanks, ponds, or recharge structures, reducing flooding, preventing soil erosion, and making efficient use of otherwise wasted rainwater.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is rainwater harvesting?

Rainwater harvesting is the collection and storage of rainwater for reuse. It reduces dependence on municipal water supply and conserves natural resources.

How does it save water?

By collecting rainwater, households and businesses can lower consumption from main supply, reduce wastage, and preserve freshwater sources.

Where can harvested water be used?

It can be used for gardening, cleaning, flushing, and in some cases for drinking after proper filtration.

What are the common techniques?

Rooftop harvesting, surface runoff collection, recharge pits, percolation tanks, and rain barrels are popular methods

Does it prevent flooding?

Yes, proper collection reduces surface runoff, minimizes urban flooding, and protects soil from erosion.

Is it suitable for homes and offices?

Absolutely, systems can be scaled for residential, commercial, or industrial use according to water demand.

Do I need maintenance?

Minimal maintenance is required, mainly cleaning filters and checking storage tanks to ensure water quality.
